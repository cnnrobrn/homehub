# Vision

**Purpose.** Describe what HomeHub is, who it serves, and why it should exist.

**Scope.** The product thesis. Not architecture, not features.

## One-sentence pitch

HomeHub is the household's shared AI control panel: it pulls each member's calendars, email, money, and activity into one place, enriches everything into a shared memory, and lets the family's AI agents summarize, warn, and coordinate across money, meals, leisure, and relationships.

## The problem

Running a household today means stitching together a dozen single-user apps: one person's calendar, another's budgeting app, someone's meal planner, a shared grocery list, and a group chat for everything else. Nothing sees across the silos. The family's collective context — who's free, what's been eaten, what's due, whose birthday is coming — lives in people's heads and slips between tools.

## The bet

A household will pay for a single pane of glass that:

1. Connects every family member's relevant accounts through a single secure broker.
2. Turns every inbound signal into structured, linked memory the family can browse.
3. Runs background agents that draft summaries, flag conflicts, and propose actions so that the household runs itself with far less active coordination.

## Success looks like

- A family can open HomeHub in the morning and see "here's the day" across all four segments without switching apps.
- "Did we ever meet the Garcias' kids?" gets a concrete answer from the memory graph, not a guess.
- The grocery run on Saturday is generated, not written by hand.
- No household member has to be the household's "operations manager."

## What HomeHub is not

- Not a replacement for the upstream tools (Google, budgeting app, etc.). It reads from and writes back to them.
- Not a chat app. Messaging belongs elsewhere; HomeHub links to it.
- Not a single-user productivity app. A solo user can use it, but the design optimizes for a multi-person household.
